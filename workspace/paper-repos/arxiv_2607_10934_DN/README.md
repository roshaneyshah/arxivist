# kyle_liquidity

**Numerical verification suite for**: *Multidimensional stochastic liquidity in Kyle's model of informed trading*
Ibrahim Ekren, Evangelos A. Nikitopoulos, Lu Vy — [arXiv:2607.10934](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10934)

> ArXivist paper_id: `arxiv_2607_10934` · Generated by the ArXivist pipeline (Stages 1–6)

## What this paper does (plain English)

The paper reformulates Kyle's classic model of insider trading — where a trader who
knows an asset's true value trades against a market maker who only sees noisy order
flow — as a variational (optimal-transport-flavored) problem, extended to *multiple*
assets and *stochastic* liquidity. The market maker's shrinking uncertainty about the
true value is treated as an inventory being "liquidated" over time; the shadow price of
that liquidation is a matrix-valued martingale `M*_t` (market depth), whose inverse is
the price-impact matrix. The main theorem is *conditional* on a technical hypothesis
(the "martingale dual condition", MDC): if a certain dual optimizer is a *true* matrix
martingale (not just a local one), an equilibrium exists. The paper proves MDC holds —
and recovers known results — in the scalar case and in a multi-asset case where the
covariance and volatility matrices share a fixed eigenbasis; the fully general
matrix-valued case is left as an open FBSDE wellposedness problem.

**This is a theory paper: there is no neural network, no dataset, and no numeric
results table.** This repo is therefore not a conventional ArXivist "reproduction" —
it is a **numerical verification suite** that simulates the paper's equilibrium
construction for every closed-form benchmark case in Section 5 and checks it against
the paper's own formulas.

## What gets verified, and against what

| Case | Paper section | What's checked |
|---|---|---|
| Kyle (1985), constant vol | 5.1 | Terminal covariance identity (eq. 4.6), terminal price revelation `P*_T → v_true` (eq. 3.11), `M*_t` stays positive |
| Back & Pedersen (1998), static info | 5.2 | Same, with deterministic time-varying `sigma(t)` |
| Collin-Dufresne & Fos (2016) | 5.4 | Terminal covariance identity checked **in expectation** via Monte Carlo over simulated stochastic-volatility paths (the closed form here is path-dependent, not just a function of `t`) |
| Common eigenbasis / Back-Cocquemas-Ekren-Lioui (2020) | 5.6 / 5.3 | The `n`-scalar-subproblem construction (eq. 5.49) is checked against the direct constant-volatility matrix closed form — a pure linear-algebra identity, machine-precision agreement expected |

## Explicitly NOT implemented (and why)

- **Section 5.7 (general, non-commuting matrix case)**: the paper states wellposedness
  of the resulting coupled matrix FBSDE is an open problem. `src/kyle_liquidity/fbsde_stub.py`
  contains only a clearly-labeled, unverified stub that raises on use — see the module
  docstring. Do not treat it as a validated result.
- **Section 5.5 (general scalar stochastic liquidity via the Yong-Zhou stochastic
  maximum principle)**: recovering this numerically requires a quadratic-BSDE solver
  (e.g. deep BSDE / Han-Jentzen-E, or LSMC) that the paper does not specify. Out of
  scope for the default suite; flagged as future work.

## Reproducibility notes / implementation assumptions

(Full list in `sir-registry/arxiv_2607_10934/sir.json -> implementation_assumptions`.)

- Time discretization: uniform grid, Euler-Maruyama for all SDEs (paper is continuous-time throughout; ASSUMED, not specified).
- The simulation grid stops at `T - eps` (`eps = 1e-6` by default) to avoid the exact
  singularity of `Sigma*_t` at `t = T`.
- Matrix inverses use an eigenvalue-floor safeguard (`1e-10`) to avoid blow-ups from
  numerical near-singularity late in the path.
- MDC (Definition 3.1) has no general numerical certificate; `min_eig_M_along_path` is
  reported as an **empirical, non-rigorous** health check only.
- `n_assets=3` and a random SPD `C` are used for the common-eigenbasis demo (arbitrary choice, not from the paper).

**Overall SIR confidence: 0.58** (below the pipeline's 0.65 human-review threshold —
see `sir-registry/arxiv_2607_10934/metadata.json`). This reflects that several SIR
sections (architecture/tensor_semantics/training_pipeline, which are ML-shaped by
default) had to be reinterpreted for a pure theory paper, and that Section 5.7 is
explicitly open in the source paper itself, not a parsing gap.
